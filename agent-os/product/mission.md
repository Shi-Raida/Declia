# Declia — Mission

## Problem

Professional photographers currently juggle 5–6 disconnected tools to run their business:
- **Galleries & delivery:** Pixieset, Pic-Time, Smugmug
- **Booking & contracts:** HoneyBook, Calendly, Dubsado
- **CRM & invoicing:** separate spreadsheets or generic tools
- **Shop (prints/products):** WHCC integrations, manual orders
- **Marketing:** Mailchimp, separate SMS tools

No single platform combines all of these. Every tool has its own login, its own data silo, its own monthly fee — and nothing talks to anything else seamlessly.

## Target Users

**Primary:** Professional photographers — initially **Fleur de Lumiere (FL / Capucine)**, a professional photographer who validated the product concept and requirements through the CDC process.

**Secondary:** Other professional photographers, acquired via SaaS subscription once the platform is genericized.

## Solution

**Declia** is an all-in-one SaaS platform built specifically for professional photographers. It combines:

- **Public showcase website** — SEO-optimized portfolio and service presentation
- **Online booking** — session reservation, mini-sessions, scolaire modules
- **Client space & galleries** — secure delivery, photo selection, before/after retouching previews
- **Integrated shop** — prints, photo objects, digital coffrets, gift cards
- **Loyalty & referral** — points system, parrainage, promotional offers
- **Marketing automation** — avant-première, abandoned cart, last-chance, gallery wake-up, anniversary campaigns
- **Full back-office** — CRM, planning, workflow automation, invoicing (Factur-X), accounting export, statistics
- **Education hub** — client-facing educational content

### Business Model

Declia is built as a multi-tenant SaaS platform from day one — not a custom project that gets generalized later. FL (Fleur de Lumiere / Capucine) is the **first subscriber**, not a special case. Every feature is designed to be generic, configurable, and reusable by any photographer.

FL's role is to validate the product with real usage. She gets a branded instance of the same platform that every future subscriber will use.
